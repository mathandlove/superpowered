# AI Power Hour - Tutoring Session with Eddy Martin

**Participants:** Elliott Hedman (AI Designer) and Eddy Martin (18-year-old sports designer)

---

## Session Overview

Elliott is an AI designer volunteering to teach students how to use AI effectively. Eddy is an 18-year-old sports/advertisement designer who designs for high schools, works with local sports teams, and is trying to build a freelance career.

---

## Key Discussion Topics

### Eddy's Background
- Sports and advertisement designer (baseball, football mainly)
- Works with high schools making schedules and game day graphics
- Has worked with a semi-pro soccer team and a UFC fighter
- Adobe certified but no college degree
- Struggling with marketing and finding new clients
- Just came back from his first job interview

### Main Challenge: Finding Clients & Building a Career

Eddy's biggest challenge is feeling "stagnated" - he has a couple of clients but struggles to grow his client base. He's good at design but not at marketing himself.

---

## AI Tutoring Lessons

### 1. Use Voice Input Instead of Typing
Elliott encouraged Eddy to use ChatGPT's microphone feature for faster, more natural conversations with AI.

### 2. Provide Context to AI
The AI jumps to solutions without understanding context. Elliott taught Eddy to share personal details:
- "I'm 18 years old"
- "I haven't sold to an actual customer before"
- "I have two clients but I'm stagnating in getting new clients"

### 3. Ask AI to "Think Critically"
Elliott: "You should tell it to think, 'cause otherwise it'll just not think, which is really funny."

### 4. Ask AI to Grade You
Elliott: "Asking it to grade you is one of the best ways to get it to give you actual information. It can't just be like 'you're doing great' - you're like 'am I? What grade?'"

### 5. Share Your Work with AI
Eddy shared his portfolio with ChatGPT to get feedback on his design work and potential as a sports designer.

### 6. Use Deep Research Feature
Eddy's biggest takeaway was learning about ChatGPT's "deep research" mode:
- Elliott: "It's so different than the kind of BS things it does all the time"
- Eddy: "That's what I'm totally going to use a lot... I do better with that type of stuff"

### 7. Be Specific About Your Constraints
When asking for examples of successful designers, Elliott taught Eddy to add constraints:
- "Focus only on people who are under 21 and do not have a college degree in design"
- Otherwise AI will reference famous designers with millions of followers which isn't helpful

### 8. Generate Many Ideas at Once
Elliott's favorite prompt technique: "Help me come up with 20 creative, unique ways of finding tiny real clients for sports design that an 18-year-old can do"
- Half won't be good, but the other half will be useful

### 9. Push Back on AI
When AI gave advice Eddy disagreed with, Elliott encouraged him to tell the AI and have a conversation about it.

---

## Elliott's Personal Advice (Beyond AI)

### Start Doing Work for Free
- "You need to start doing it for free"
- Reach out to every high school coach and offer to help build your portfolio
- "If it's free, they don't care. There's no risk."
- Ask for reviews and testimonials in return

### Build Credibility Over Quick Cash
- "The credibility is what will actually get you paid later on"
- "You're not actually trying to get a paycheck - you're trying to build relationships"
- Elliott did free projects for Hasbro and video game companies, then people started paying thousands

### Reach Out with Specific Offers
- Don't just ask "will you hire me?" - nobody responds to that
- Instead: "I noticed your website doesn't have social marketing. Would you be open to me helping you build that out? I'm doing it for free."
- Use ChatGPT to help draft outreach messages

### Network with Successful People
- Use deep research to find successful sports designers under 21 without degrees
- Reach out to them: "I like the career you're building. Could I chat with you about how you achieved it?"
- "Everyone wants to help out"
